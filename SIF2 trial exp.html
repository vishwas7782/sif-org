About our Founder: Evg Ashok Kumar

Our founder is Evg Ashok Kumar Gupta. He was born and brought-up in a small village of Bihar, Karnaul Garh- a village in Sahebganj block of Muzaffarpur district, Bihar. He has done his education in Assam. He was doing a running his own business and their after a job when God called him for hi full time ministry in the year 2007. From the 2007 to the year 2015 he and his family did the service of God in Motipur. In the year 2005 God lead them to their native place, Sahebganj, for the ministry and they mod=ved to Sahebganj. Now he Kumar and his family are staying at Sahebganj and serving the Lord with whole family. Evg. Ashok Kumar had a desire to open a free school for the ragpicker children. Whenver he used to see them picking rags for their survival, he felt very bad for them. When he talked to them some of them told their they don't have father, some told that they don't have their mother. That's why they are doing this for their survival because no one is there to look after them and if they will not do this then they will not get food. They picks the rags fromn the heap of garbages. They usually jump into the garbage heap and then picks some items which can be sold to some junk stores. They barely gets the money sufficient for the food. Sometimes they need to beg for getting food. When Evg Ashok Kumar show this, he felt very bad for them and started to pray for them continuously. He has burden to start a school for them. So, that they can have a better future. This became the base for the Shalom India Foundation. While working for these children we need to provide education to them, financial support to their family so that they will not send them to do the old work again.

We here at Shalom India Foundation works for the betterment of the human life. Today when we look around in our society we find a huge number of children who are not getting proper education, proper healthcare facilities, proper food, clothes and many other basic things necessary for every human being. We at Salom India Foundation try to show some love to the children who lack this facilities, as our Christ loved us, we spread His love to others. We know that we being a small foundation,and we are also a human being, can't change the life of everyone. But, if our small step brings happiness in the life of even one chil or one family, we will be more than happy to do this. In our own country India, at least 15 crore children and youths are out of the country's formal education system, and roughly about 25 crore population is below the primary definition of literacy. Over 53% children in India under five years - that is, 67 million - live without basic healthcare facilities. Aabout 53 per cent of children in India are not growing well which means they are either stunted or wasted or overweight or a combination of these. Over 30 per cent of children in India are stunted while 17.3 per cent of children are wasted, which is highest in the Asia-Pacific region. Almost 1.6 per cent of children in India are overweight or are too heavy for their height. Just 42 per cent of children who are 6–23 months of age are fed the required number of times per day in our country We should Think about this seriously.
